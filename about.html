Dear members of our community,

On September 19, 2021, Mother’s Knowledge was established by Geetaponkalan Peter and Sudan Arumugam in loving memory of Kanakambikai Kandasamy Amma. We extend a heartfelt invitation to our Tamil brethren residing in Sri Lanka and across the globe to join us in steering our community towards a trajectory of progress and development. For years, concerns have lingered regarding the educational plight of Tamil students. The enduring concern of how to initiate change may have persisted in your thoughts. Today, we stand united with our fellow Tamil community members, ready to take these aspirations into action. G Tamils Focus stands shoulder to shoulder with you, committed to facilitating educational advancement for Tamil students residing across diverse regions, including the Northern and Eastern Highlands, as well as Colombo. Recognizing the multifaceted needs of our community, encompassing economic upliftment, livelihood enhancement, and cultural preservation, we endeavor to cultivate a vibrant society that embraces change. Mother’s Knowledge, led by G Tamils Focus, has been established not only in academic institutions like Vavuniya University, Phungudutheivu, and Killinochi but also in various temples. Our primary goal is to ensure that every Tamil student has unhindered access to education, irrespective of financial constraints. You can play a pivotal role in shaping the future of these students by contributing to Mother’s Knowledge. Whether it's commemorating milestones such as anniversaries, birthdays, or personal successes, your donations can make a significant difference in the lives of these students. Mother’s Knowledge, initiated by G Tamils Focus, has transformed into a platform dedicated to fostering a culture of educational philanthropy. Here, you have the chance to make a meaningful contribution, not just by providing fundamental supplies like pencils, pens, and books, but also by generously donating electronic devices. Our donation program extends beyond conventional educational materials to encompass vital electronic equipment, thus enabling students without access to such resources to bridge the digital gap. Our vision is for a Tamil society where a farmer's son can aspire to become a doctor and a maid’s daughter can dream of becoming an engineer. Poverty should not serve as a barrier to educational excellence, and we implore your support in eliminating this obstacle and enabling access to education for all.
